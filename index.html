Tom Griffin's blog.

My name is Tom Griffin and I am in Auckland. I’m looking forward to joining my Horoeka cohort in person in October :crossed_fingers:
I was going for a run last Sunday and happened to see the CEO of the company I had just resigned from on Friday. I said I had resigned to attend a Development Bootcamp and he said in a CEO-kind-of-way ‘Follow your dreams!’ I thought to myself - that’s a bit strong. I haven’t literally dreamed of doing a Development Bootcamp!
But in a CEO-kind-of-way he was right. 20 years ago I went to Auckland Uni to do computer science. I’ve since worked in the tech industry in QA roles and management roles which were awesome. But, I originally wanted to do be a Developer and that ‘dream’ never completely left me. About 6 weeks ago, I was feeling my time was coming to an end at my then-current job so I made a quick decision to resign in time to be able to start at EDA with you all on Monday.
I’m at EDA to learn some new skills and new ways of thinking. I’m at EDA because after the first COVID lockdown, I realised my family could survive challenges, and that if you don’t follow your dreams now, you might miss out. Why EDA? I really like the look of the Tech and Core components of the course, and I have a friend who went through EDA 2 years ago and loved it. Regarding Values and Identity - at the moment figuring out my values seems a bit out of reach for me. Thinking about and changing my identity seems more relevant and interesting to me.
