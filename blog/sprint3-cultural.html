IQ represents abilities such as;

  • Visual and spatial processing
  • Knowledge of the world
  • Fluid reasoning
  • Working memory and short-term memory
  • Quantitative reasoning

EQ refers to a person's ability to perceive, control, evaluate, and express emotions.

  • Identifying emotions
  • Controlling one's own emotions
  • Using emotions to facilitate social communication
  • Perceiving how others feel
  • Evaluating how others feel
  • Relating to others

Why EQ is important and how it is relevant to me.

For me, it's very important to build my skills at identifying and managing my own emotions. Without intentionally doing this, my self-critic can take over and I develop some negative/pessimistic perspectives, which then affects my mood, confidence short term and long term.

It feels really weird to stop and try to identify my emotions - it seems much easier to push them down and get on with my work. EQ is like applying your IQ to your and other people's emotions.

The world is a changeable, complex, uncertain and ambiguous place.
____It's hard to be a human

So how can we make the best of it?
____Develop EQ skills to monitor own/other's emotions and use these to guide my thinking and actions
