My Learning plan.

What is your long term goal and or career pathway?

I want to spend 2-3 years in Front End Development or mobile. - I basically want to spend 2-3 years programming to build my skills and self-confidence. - FED attracts me the most because I want to be able to see something tangible from my efforts and I am intrinsically motivated by design issues. - But actually it is my craft I want to develop. So I'd rather work in a good backend team than a FED team with poor craft - From there I'll decide to either go further up the Tech ladder, or swap over to Team Lead. - I'm pretty scared of it, but longest-term, I'd like to run a small Eng dept, whether that's my own startup or not, I'd like to have some shares in it one way or another.

What skills (non-technical - human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at EDA?

Emotional Intelligence skills. - When I'm feeling vulnerable in some way, I get self-critical. This can impact my work, my relationship with colleagues etc. - I want to develop thinking patterns to recognise and neutralise the self-critic. - And I'd like a second _nice-to-have_ set of skills like walking into a room and instead of feeling anxious, wish everyone happiness. - Time Management skills - I've had good chats with my facilitator already about these issues. - Tech topics/activities are an infinite well. I want to develop skills to go as deep as I need to and no further. - I love the metaphor of committing meaningful work small-and-often and I want to apply it to how I manage my time. - Break work down to the smallest meaningful, actionable pieces. - Do those to get regular sense of satisfaction. - Commit regularly so I know where I'm up to and don't need to keep state in my head. Plus I can look back and see where I've been so my Remembering self doesn't become too critical. - With small pieces of work and regular commits, it is easy to share where I got up to with my teammates.

#### Limitations - My ego. I can be afraid to look bad i.e. asking for help, submitting work I subjectively think is poor. - Overly emotionally invested in work. Sometimes I can't just walk away from a project that isn't done. I can borrow too heavily from my energy/happiness and I'll end up paying for it later with a low mood/tiredness.

#### Strengths - I enjoy collaborating and helping others to succeed. - I'm interested in a wide range of topics in the Bootcamp. - I have a good eye for visual design - I'd rather do something small and complete it than the opposite, when working in teams. - I'm constrained by commitments to family so am disciplined
